Manila City

Attractions

Manila Ocean Park: Manila Ocean Park is an oceanarium and marine-themed park featuring a variety of marine life exhibits, interactive shows, and the opportunity to experience swimming with dolphins.

Intramuros: Also known as the "Walled City," Intramuros is the historic heart of Manila. It features well-preserved Spanish colonial architecture, including Fort Santiago, San Agustin Church, and other historical sites.

San Agustin Church: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, San Agustin Church is the oldest stone church in the Philippines. It is a masterpiece of Baroque architecture and houses various religious artifacts.

Fort Santiago: a historical citadel located in Intramuros, Manila, Philippines. It is a defense fortress built during the Spanish colonial period to protect the city of Manila. Fort Santiago has a triangular plan, with bastions at its corners. The walls are made of stone, and there is a moat surrounding the fort. The design follows the principles of Renaissance military architecture.

Manila Zoo: Manila Zoo is situated in the district of Malate, Manila, near Rizal Park and the Cultural Center of the Philippines. The zoo features a variety of animals, including mammals, birds, reptiles, and marine species. Some of the notable animals include elephants, tigers, lions, zebras, and a diverse range of local and exotic species.

Cultural Center of the Philippines (CCP): A venue for various performing arts, CCP hosts concerts, theater productions, and art exhibits. The complex includes the Tanghalang Pambansa (National Theater) and other cultural facilities.


Restaurants

Ristorante Delle Mitre: Situated in Intramuros, this restaurant is known for its historical ambiance and serves a variety of Filipino and Spanish dishes. A Catholic-themed restaurant just beside San Agustin Church in Intramuros—Ristorante delle Mitre serves various culinary dishes but is also a mini-museum showcasing historical photos and artifacts for its interiors.

8065 Bagnet: the casual eatery is all about crispy-fried, succulent bagnet, which they use in a variety of dishes. Try their Sinigang Bagnet, which will get you crispy pork in a lip-smacking sour soup. The Kare Kare Bagnet with bagnet blanketed in the rich peanut sauce of the classic Filipino dish is also a must-order that might get you ordering extra rice. If you’re on a budget, you’ll especially love that solo meals here only go for P145 to P150.

Ramon Lee’s Panciteria: Practically an institution in Santa Cruz, Ramon Lee’s Panciteria has been cooking up their signature fried chicken for more than 90 years now—since 1929. Their chicken is fried Chinese-style, with no heavy batter but a crisp, deep-fried skin that gives way to juicy meat. Pair your chicken with other Chinese-Filipino staples

Cafe Ilang-IlangThe classic Manila restaurant that everyone who can, should try at least once. Whether it’s dining through ala carte options like the sinigang or the crispy pata or the famous Cafe Ilang-Ilang buffet you’re sure to be satisfied each and every time

Bistro Remedios: Bistro Remedios is one of the older restaurants of The LJC Group (also behind Fely J’s and Abe), first opening in 1984 at the corner of Remedios and Adriatico Streets before moving to its current location along Adriatico. Decorated like a Filipino ancestral home, Bistro Remedios is the perfect venue for your next family reunion—plus they offer Filipino classics in hefty, shareable servings. Have their signature Sinigang na Bangus sa Bayabas and Hipon Binondo or fresh shrimp sauteed with sliced green chili.


Landmarks

Manila Baywalk: If you want to see the best views in Manila, don’t miss out on visiting the Manila Baywalk. Situated along Roxas Boulevard, tourists will get a chance to witness the most spectacular sunset view when strolling through the Manila Baywalk. This two-kilometer stretch is a popular location for art performances, jogging, and even just strolling. Within the area, you will also find alfresco cafes and restaurants.

Binondo District: Known as the "Chinatown of Manila," Binondo district is a bustling commercial area that dates back to the Spanish colonization era. It is the oldest district famous for its street foods, the 17th-century Minor Basilica of San Lorenzo Ruiz, and the Lucky China Town Mall. You can also take a walking tour around Binondo church and ancient ruins and learn about the district's history.

Manila City Hall is the official seat of government of the City of Manila, located in the historic center of Ermita, Manila. It is where the Mayor of Manila holds office and the chambers of the Manila City Council is located.

Quiapo Church: The Minor Basilica of the Black Nazarene, or Quiapo Church, is one of Manila's oldest churches. Built in 1588 by Spanish friars, this church is home to a miraculous statue of Jesus Christ known as the Black Nazarene. Every Friday, thousands gather together for the “Procession” and make their way to the church for a mass.


Mall

Greenhills Shopping Center: Greenhills also said to be the shopper's paradise, is very famous in Manila. When in Greenhills, you will find all the kinds of stores from affordable to high end shops. Small retail stalls offering clothing items are available where you can even bargain. This mall is a modern construction with salons, wellness centres, fancy restaurants, theatres, music cinemas and more. This mall also trades in one of the best and original Pearls from the South sea.

168 Shopping Mall: Located on the busy streets of Binondo, 168 Shopping Mall is like a shopping centre with the benefits of bargaining. Who does not like to bargain? 168 Shopping Mall has many retail shops and outlets selling clothing, hardware, jewellery, souvenirs, decor items at low and affordable rates. This mall is very popular among the locals and is also a must-visit for the foreign tourists.

SM City Manila: SM City Manila is one of the SM Group stores that can be found all around the Philippines (and even in China). It features all the SM chain-businesses such as SM Supermarket, SM Appliance Center, SM Cinemas and SM Food Court, as well as many chain stores such as Watson’s, National Bookstore, plus many independent boutiques and restaurants.

Lucky Chinatown: Lucky Chinatown in Binondo is one of the popular shopping complexes in Manila. This place has a very friendly environment with gardens and an open space dining area. It owns international fashion brands and also budget shopping brands. You will also find fitness and beauty centres here.


National Parks

Rizal Park: Rizal Park, formerly known as Bagumbayan, is not only that space where the Rizal Monument stands. The sprawling 58-hectare urban park also features the Children's Garden at the southeastern corner, the Chinese Garden with its ornate Chinese-style gate, the Japanese Garden with a lagoon and a bridge, and the Noli Me Tangere Garden that features a fountain donated by Germany, said to be one that Rizal would drink from during his stay there.

Arroceros Forest Park: Arroceros Forest Park, which is often dubbed the "last lung of Manila," was a greening project initiated by members of Winner Foundation in collaboration with former Manila mayor Alfredo Lim and then first lady Amelita Ramos in 1993

Mehan Garden: Mehan Garden, originally established as Jardin Botanico by the Spaniards in 1858, was transformed into a public park during the American occupation. It was reopened to the public in 2019 following cleaning operations, installation of lighting, and rehabilitation.


Unique Stays

The hotel features a restaurant and a 24-hour front desk. The rooms at CityState Tower Hotel are all air-conditioned and are equipped with a saftey deposit box, ironing facilities, and a seating area.

Manila Manor Hotel: The Manila Manor Hotel is located in Manila within 3.7 km from Intramuros and 5.9 km from SM Mall of Asia, featuring an on site restaurant, a 24-hour front desk, and massage services available all day. Free WiFi is also available throughout the property. All rooms come with air conditioning, a cable TV, and a minibar.

The Bayleaf Intramuros: Located within the historic walled city of Intramuros, The Bayleaf is a 5-minute walk from SM Manila and 1.6 km from Fort Santiago. It features a rooftop restaurant and bar, free parking, and free WiFi throughout the property. Featuring different colour motifs, the air-conditioned rooms come with a flat-screen cable TV, a safety deposit box, and a working desk.

Amelie Hotel Manila: Amelie Hotel Manila is a modern hotel offering air-conditioned bedrooms with free WiFi in Manila. It has its own restaurant, fitness centre, a swimming pool and an indoor roof deck event space. A 3-minute walk from Robinson's Place Manila, Amelie Hotel is a 5-minute drive to Manila Ocean Park and Rizal Park.
